Mission

The CCS2110 College of Computer Studies is committed to:

  • Delivering high-quality undergraduate and continuing education programs in computing and information technology that balance theory and practical application.
  • Fostering innovation and problem-solving through project-based learning, community engagement, and strong industry partnerships.
  • Promoting the ethical, inclusive, and sustainable use of technology by developing professional responsibility and digital citizenship among students.
  • Supporting research and development initiatives that address real-world challenges and contribute to regional and national growth.

Core Values

  • Integrity – We uphold honesty, transparency, and ethical conduct in all aspects of learning and research.
  • Excellence – We pursue continuous improvement in teaching, learning, and service delivery.
  • Innovation – We encourage creativity, experimentation, and technology-driven solutions.
  • Collaboration – We build meaningful partnerships across disciplines, industries, and communities.

Strategic Goals

To fulfill its mission, CCS2110 College of Computer Studies aims to:

  • Enhance and continuously update the computing curriculum with emerging technologies such as Artificial Intelligence, Cloud Computing, and Mobile Development.
  • Strengthen partnerships with industry to provide internship, mentorship, and collaborative project opportunities for students and faculty.
  • Promote research, innovation, and community-based projects that address social and technological challenges.
  • Improve infrastructure and access to advanced computing facilities and resources.
  • Support faculty development programs to ensure up-to-date technical knowledge and pedagogical excellence.
  • Foster a culture of inclusivity, ethics, and professionalism in technology practice.
  • Increase graduate employability by aligning learning outcomes with current and future IT industry demands.
