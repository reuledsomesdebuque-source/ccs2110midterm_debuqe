History

Founded in 1991, CCS2110 began as a small computer training center offering short courses in programming, data processing, and basic computer literacy. During its early years, the institution operated with just two classrooms, a handful of computers, and a team of passionate educators who believed in the transformative power of technology.

By the mid-2000s, the college had expanded its curriculum to include degree programs in Computer Science, Information Systems, and Information Technology. This marked a major milestone as CCS2110 gained recognition from academic and industry partners for producing competent graduates ready to meet the demands of the growing IT sector.

Today, CCS2110 College of Computer Studies stands as a recognized center for innovation and digital learning. With state-of-the-art laboratories, industry-linked research initiatives, and community engagement programs, the college continues its mission to empower students to lead in the ever-evolving digital world - a legacy built from humble beginnings in 1991 and strengthened through decades of commitment to academic excellence and technological advancement.
